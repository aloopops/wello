Sensor Suite

Nine sensors.
Every signal that matters.

Beneath the minimal woven exterior lies medical-grade hardware that most clinical devices reserve for hospital settings. Here is everything Wello measures — and why it matters to you.

Continuous Heart Rate

50 – 220 bpm

Multi-wavelength PPG (photoplethysmography) sensors on the underside of the band emit green, red, and infrared light into your wrist capillaries and measure the returning signal. This gives Wello an accurate heartbeat reading every second, 24 hours a day — not just during workouts.

Healthy resting heart rate: 50–80 bpm. A sustained resting rate above 90 bpm may indicate elevated stress or early illness.

Heart Rate Variability

Nightly · 20 – 120 ms

HRV is measured automatically during your deepest sleep window — the phase where parasympathetic activity peaks and readings are most reliable. Wello uses RMSSD methodology (the clinical standard) to compute the millisecond variation between successive heartbeats. This single number is the most sensitive indicator of recovery, stress load, and cardiovascular health.

HRV is highly individual. Wello tracks your personal baseline over weeks to detect meaningful deviation, not absolute comparison.

Blood Oxygen (SpO2)

70 – 100 %

Red and infrared LEDs measure how much light is absorbed by your haemoglobin. Oxygenated blood absorbs more infrared light; deoxygenated blood absorbs more red. The ratio gives a continuous SpO2 percentage. Wello flags sustained drops below 90% and generates a nightly respiratory report for overnight desaturation events.

Healthy SpO2: 95–100%. Altitude, sleep apnea, and certain conditions can cause drops Wello will flag.

Sleep Stage Analysis

Deep · REM · Light · Awake

Wello combines accelerometer movement data, HRV patterns, heart rate, and SpO2 to classify your sleep into four stages throughout the night. Deep sleep governs physical tissue repair; REM governs memory consolidation and emotional regulation. Wello tracks time in each stage and provides a composite Sleep Score nightly.

87% concordance with clinical polysomnography (PSG) in internal validation studies across 340 nights of data.

Skin Temperature

Relative deviation · ±2 °C

A high-sensitivity thermistor on the band back measures skin temperature to 0.05°C accuracy throughout the night. Rather than reporting an absolute number, Wello tracks your nightly deviation from your personal baseline. An upward deviation of 0.5°C or more often predicts illness 12–24 hours before symptomatic onset. For women, this data also maps precisely onto the luteal phase of the menstrual cycle.

Skin temperature trends establish a personal baseline over 2–3 weeks. The accuracy of illness and cycle prediction improves over time.

Stress Monitoring

Physiological load index

Wello models physiological stress by combining daytime HRV suppression, heart rate elevation above baseline, breathing rate, and movement variability. This is distinct from perceived stress — the band detects the body's autonomic response to stress, not just your subjective feeling. High-stress periods are highlighted in your daily timeline to correlate with your schedule and habits.

Chronic high stress manifests as consistently suppressed HRV. Wello trends this over weeks, not just days.

Women's Wellness

Cycle · Ovulation · Luteal

Using continuous nightly temperature data, Wello tracks the biphasic temperature shift that marks ovulation — temperatures rise 0.2–0.5°C post-ovulation and remain elevated through the luteal phase. This allows cycle-phase predictions, PMS pattern identification, and retrospective ovulation confirmation. All data is local and never shared or monetised.

Temperature-based cycle tracking becomes statistically reliable after 2–3 full cycles of data collection.

Blood Pressure Trends

Relative PTT tracking

Using Pulse Transit Time (PTT) — the delay between the heartbeat signal and its arrival at the wrist sensor — Wello can detect relative changes in arterial stiffness and blood pressure over time. This is not a cuff-equivalent measurement, but it is a validated method for detecting meaningful upward trends in cardiovascular load, which often precede clinical hypertension by months.

PTT-based BP trending is a screening tool, not a diagnostic replacement for regular cuff measurements.

Recovery & Readiness

0 – 100 daily score

Wello synthesises all overnight metrics into a single Readiness Score each morning. The algorithm weighs HRV vs. your baseline (40%), sleep quality and duration (30%), resting heart rate trend (20%), and skin temperature deviation (10%). Scores above 70 indicate high readiness; below 50 suggests active recovery or an early stress response.

Readiness Scores become more personalised and accurate as Wello builds your multi-week physiological baseline.
