Heart Rate Variability

The milliseconds between beats reveal everything.

A healthy heart does not beat like a metronome. The time between each beat — called an R-R interval — varies constantly in response to your autonomic nervous system. This variation is Heart Rate Variability, and it is one of the most sensitive biomarkers in human physiology.

When your parasympathetic nervous system (rest-and-digest) dominates — as it does during deep sleep and genuine recovery — HRV is high. When your sympathetic system (fight-or-flight) dominates — during stress, illness, overtraining, or poor sleep — HRV drops measurably.

Wello uses the RMSSD method (Root Mean Square of Successive Differences), which is the clinical standard endorsed by the European Society of Cardiology. RMSSD is measured during your deepest sleep window, when external noise is lowest and the parasympathetic signal is strongest.

Why it matters: A single HRV reading means little. Wello builds your personal baseline over 2–4 weeks, then alerts you to meaningful deviations — which correlate strongly with overtraining, illness onset, and chronic stress before you feel symptoms.

Sleep Architecture

Every stage of sleep serves a different purpose.

Sleep is not a single uniform state. Your brain cycles through four distinct stages approximately every 90 minutes throughout the night, each serving a different biological function. Missing even one stage has measurable consequences the next day.

Awake (5–10%)

Brief micro-awakenings are normal. Frequent interruptions lasting over 2 minutes degrade deep and REM accumulation.

Light Sleep (40–55%)

The transition phase. Sleep spindles during Light sleep are linked to motor memory consolidation and sensory processing. Also when your body temperature drops and melatonin peaks.

Deep Sleep (15–25%)

Slow-wave sleep is when physical repair happens — muscle tissue rebuilds, the glymphatic system flushes metabolic waste from the brain, and growth hormone is released. This is your body's maintenance window.

REM Sleep (20–25%)

Rapid Eye Movement sleep is when emotional regulation, long-term memory formation, and creative processing occur. Most REM accumulates in the final two hours of a full night — which is why cutting sleep short disproportionately hurts cognition.

Wello detects stages using a fusion of accelerometer movement patterns (you're almost motionless in Deep sleep), heart rate variability signatures (HRV peaks in Deep and REM), and SpO2 breathing patterns — cross-validating three independent signals for each stage classification.

The Screenless Argument

The most accurate data comes when you forget the device is there.

Smartwatches solve a fundamental tension poorly: they are supposed to improve your health while simultaneously demanding your attention. A screen creates interaction loops — check your steps, dismiss the notification, glance at the time. Each interaction fragment activates the sympathetic nervous system slightly, nudging your HRV down.

Wello eliminates this contradiction. No haptic buzz at 2 AM. No glowing screen in a dark room. No mid-workout notification. The band is passive — collecting data with zero interface overhead — which also makes it a significantly better data collector.
